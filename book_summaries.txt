## Title: 1984
A dystopian story about a totalitarian society controlled by surveillance, propaganda, and thought police. Winston Smith, the protagonist, secretly rebels against the system in search of truth and freedom. Themes: control, freedom, manipulation, resistance.

## Title: The Hobbit
Bilbo Baggins, a comfort-loving hobbit, is swept into an adventure to reclaim a dwarven treasure guarded by the dragon Smaug. Along the way, he discovers courage and resourcefulness he never knew he had. Themes: friendship, adventure, courage, self-discovery.

## Title: To Kill a Mockingbird
Scout Finch grows up in a racially divided Southern town, witnessing her father Atticus defend a black man falsely accused of a crime. The story explores justice, empathy, and moral growth. Themes: justice, racism, childhood, empathy.

## Title: Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone
Harry discovers he is a wizard and attends Hogwarts, where he makes friends and faces the dark wizard Voldemort. The book is filled with magic, friendship, and the battle between good and evil. Themes: magic, friendship, bravery, identity.

## Title: Pride and Prejudice
Elizabeth Bennet navigates issues of manners, marriage, and morality in 19th-century England, challenging social norms and finding love. Themes: love, class, reputation, independence.

## Title: The Catcher in the Rye
Holden Caulfield recounts his experiences in New York after being expelled from school, struggling with alienation and the loss of innocence. Themes: alienation, innocence, identity, rebellion.

## Title: The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring
Frodo Baggins embarks on a perilous journey to destroy a powerful ring, joined by a fellowship of friends. The story is about sacrifice, friendship, and the fight against evil. Themes: friendship, sacrifice, good vs. evil, courage.

## Title: Animal Farm
A group of farm animals overthrow their human owner, only to see their society become as oppressive as the one they replaced. The novella is an allegory about power and corruption. Themes: power, corruption, equality, revolution.

## Title: The Book Thief
Liesel, a young girl in Nazi Germany, finds solace in stealing books and sharing them with others, while the horrors of war unfold around her. Themes: war, friendship, the power of words, loss.

## Title: Brave New World
In a futuristic society obsessed with control and pleasure, individuality is suppressed. Bernard Marx questions the system, seeking meaning beyond comfort. Themes: control, technology, individuality, conformity.
